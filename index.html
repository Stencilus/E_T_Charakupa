Research

Undergraduate research

Effects of exchange rate and interest rates changes on loan repayments

One of the most significant revenue-generating sectors in banking is the loan book, yet credit departments face a critical challenge: borrowers’ inability to repay. This study examined how macroeconomic changes—primarily exchange rates and interest rates—affected loan repayments in Zimbabwe from 2019 to 2021 using a quantitative survey approach with lending department employees at selected banks.

Findings indicated that exchange rates, high interest rates, GDP levels, and unemployment significantly impact loan repayments. The study recommends aligning interest-rate adjustments (especially during currency depreciation) with borrower income conditions to avoid excessive repayment difficulties.
